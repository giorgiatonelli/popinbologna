POP MUSIC IN BOLOGNA

Bologna and music constitute an inseparable pair. Bologna was one of the first cities spreading jazz culture in the country, but it is also home to the most important children's festival Lo Zecchino D'Oro . Bologna is arguably one of Italy's song-writing capitals, having inspired Lucio Dalla, Francesco Guccini, Cesare Cremonini and Samuele Bersani among others. Nevertheless, not only jazz, rock and hip-hop, but also Italian pop music owes Bologna a great deal.
Pop Music a Bologna

OUR PROJECT

The present work is aimed at analysing pop bands phenomenon in Italy, with a particular focus on the scena bolognese. A corpus-based, diachronic approach has been adopted: we chose a band emblematic of each decade, thus Pooh are representative of the late 60's and 70's. Stadio represent the 80's. Lunapop are emblematic of the late 90's and early 2000s. Whereas Lo Stato Sociale is emblematic of the 2010's. Moreover, particular attention has been accorded to the works of Cesare Cremonini, Lunapop's frontman, in light of his impactful career on Italian pop music. Moreover, a reflection has been conducted on the process of producing music, hence the study on recording studios who have inspired said artists. Not only have theatres always contributed to the cultural turmoil of the city, but they also represent a cherished venue for bolognese artists, hence, the study on theatres in Italy and in Bologna more specifically.

STADIO

Stadio is an Italian band which was formed in Bologna a decade later, in 1977. They started out as Lucio Dalla’s supporting band, who featured them in his Anidride Solforosa album. Their breakthrough arrived when they produced Verdone’s Acqua e Sapone (1983) soundtrack. They worked with artists covering an essential role in the scena bolognese such as Lucio Dalla, Gianni Morandi, Francesco Guccini, Vasco Rossi and Luca Carboni . They won Sanremo Music Festival in 2016, with song Un giorno mi dirai.

CESARE CREMONINI and LUNAPOP

Cesare Cremonini respresents a key figure in the Italian music field. He was scouted with his band, Lunapop, while he was still attending high school. Lunapop's debut album, Squerez, was in fact published the summer right after Cremonini graduated Lunapop formed in 1999 and split up in 2002. Thus,frontman Cesare Cremonini pursued a career in music along with bassist Nicola "Ballo" Balestri. Lunapop won the Festival di San Marino with song Qualcosa di Grande, which allowed them to be scouted by an indie label, which produced their album Squerez. Their debut single, 50 Special, has become an Italian cult. Other singles include Un giorno migliore , Se ci sarai , Resta con Me and Vorrei .
