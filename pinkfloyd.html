A project by Alessia Perrone and Martina Santarini

A unique journey with Wikidata to discover one of the most renowned British music bands of all time.

We started our investigation analysing the number of British music bands. In particular, we focused on the rock genre as we are really interested in, and of course we could not help but to center deeply on probably the most renowned and influencing music band of all time: Pink Floyd. They became known for their biting political, social and emotional commentary.

The band was born back to 1963 thanks to the meeting of three architecture students from the University of London, Roger Waters, Nick Mason e Richard Wright. At first, the band did not have the unique name with which everyone knows them, but they changed it several times: for example, they used to call themselves Spectrum Five, Tea Set, etc. Finally, they switched to Pink Floyd in 1965, combining the names of two bluesmen, Pink Anderson and Floyd Council.

Since the beginning of their live exhibitions, Pink Floyd introduced the Sound and Vision idea, meaning an innovative experience from both a musical and visual perspective. This was a reference point for all the subsequent bands. In fact, Pink Floyd were the first ones in UK to what is known as light show, in which the projection of lights and laser play an active role in the whole show. Moreover, they were the first group to bring their own lighting system during their concerts, apart from introducing the azimuth Co-ordinator and the holophone diffusion system.

Pink Floyd began their career at the vanguard of London's underground music scene. They rapidly achieved great success and they were always keen on exploring more into various music genres. In fact, they were the founders of two major music movements: psychedelic space-rock and blues-based progressive rock.

Pink Floyd are known for their wide range of instruments, which belong to different genres and are combined harmoniously to create their songs. Moreover, it is notorious their use of non-musical sounds, a distinctive feature of the band. Pink Floyd are masters in creating atmospheres, anticipating situations, and giving sensations.

Curiosities

Pink Floyd logo

Pink Floyd owns many graphic symbols because, for the band members, designing emblems was once as important an aspect of creativity as making music. However, until 1985 they did not have a specific logo. Pictures and test elements from the covers of new albums, were used as temporary signs. In 1985 bassist Roger Waters left the band, and the other bandmates decided to have an official emblem, so they created a monogram symbol, in which the P, F, D, and G were encrypted.

Cover of The Dark Side of The Moon

The most memorable image of Pink Floyd was a triangular prism through which a ray passed, refracting and decaying into the entire spectrum of colors. This drawing became the main decoration of The Dark Side of The Moon. It was chosen to celebrate the light show that Pink Floyd performed during their concerts. The triangle, a geometric figure rich in symbolic meanings including thought and ambition, best represented the texts of Rogers Waters.
