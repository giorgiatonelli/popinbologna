POP IN BOLOGNA (Giorgia Tonelli - Camilla Guarneri)

METHODOLOGY:

A top-down approach has been adopted, meaning that we started from a general analysis concerning music genres in Bologna, we studied Italian pop bands as a whole, and then we focused on the scena bolognese. In doing so, we combined different tools.
For instance, we conducted an analysis on musicians featured in the Polifonia MusicBo Catalogue which we compared with the results obtained from using DBpedia Snorql Queries to find musicians from Bologna.
Moreover, we used Wikidata Query Service to test our queries before retrieving data from Melody. On Melody we compared the number of Italian bands with bands from Bologna. Results showed that 2088 Italian bands exist on Melody, whereas 0 bands from Bologna are registered. We retrieved data about when were bands formed, a diachronic approach has therefore been adopted in order to investigate influential pop bands from Bologna, starting from the 60's. Pooh, Stadio, Lunapop and Lo Stato Sociale proved to be the most representative of their time. Melody proved to be an essential tool to illustrate the members of each band. When presenting bands on the website, we decided to include a link to their Wikipedia page, as well as a plug-in allowing us to play their music on Spotify.
Moreover, a timeline concerning each of these bands discography has been included. Checkboxes concerning a quiz on each band have been featured on the website, in order to allow for an active reading of the article.

Moreover, Melody map feature proved essential in retrieving and visually representing data concerning recording studios and theatres. Not only, have theatres been investigated further on Melody, by analysing theatres in Bologna, but a study on theatres has also been conducted on the MusicBo catalogue.
The MusicBo catalogue has proved essential to investigate the use of musical insturments such as guitars and pianos.
We firts drafted a story on Melody, but we kept working on it allowing us to take inspiration in our daily lives, for instance, we got inspired by Cesare Cremonini's initiative to use his touring lights to create a suggestive ambiance under San Luca porticoes. We decided to link his instagram page to our story on Melody.
The website started to be developed right after we first drafted our Melody story. W3 schools and GitHub are the website that were used to this end. Initially, only the HTML language was used, it was crucial to actually have a website before being able to improve its design. The HTML was easy to master since it had been taught in class. Eventually, further research proved essential to apply the CSS language, which was important to create a navigation bar and a more accessible website. Javascript proved to be useful when creating a slideshow quoting musicians describing the city of Bologna.

PINK FLOYD (Alessia Perrone - Martina Santarini)

For our investigation, we started from some generic data concerning how many music bands, and in particular rock bands, exist in the British scenario, and then we have focused on the famous band Pink Floyd, analysing some data and information that we had access to through some different platforms and tools that we used.
At first, we explored the MusicBo corpus using the Polifonia interrogation tool. We conducted searches for pertinent terms in the music domain, ultimately focusing on the lemma 'music', ‘band’, ‘rock', and 'influence'. However, we decided to focus principally on Wikidata Query Service to test our queries before retrieving data from Melody, as it was a more useful instrument for the kind of investigation we wanted to conduct.
In the first place, we created a Melody story on the Melody website using knowledge graphs. We used Sparql queries to search the database, which was Wikidata: here we searched for pre-established codes and we added them to our search queries following the examples given during the lessons on the site of Wikidata query. Once the query was checked on Wikidata we added it on the Melody website, choosing a logic order for the data and displaying it with different visual templates in order to make the reading of the story more interesting. In particular, we used functions such as counters, tables, doughnut charts and text boxes.
In particular, we retrieved data about when the band formed, about the various components from the band and information regarding their role and personal data; we then investigated on the main musical genres played, adding the possibility for each of these to have access to the names of other bands that were represented in the same musical genre. Through further insights, we had access to data regarding the instruments played, the albums released and their musical masterpieces. Finally, we provided information regarding the Awards they won throughout their career and the number of followers they have on social media.
Once we collected all the data and composed our Melody story, we moved on to write our article on the website. For this purpose, we used W3schools to combine HTML codes with our data: we followed the tutorials provided on the website and changed the default codes accordingly to add some interesting functions to our article. In order to allow an interactive, entertaining and stimulating reading of the article both for casual listeners of the band and of course for passionate fans, we added a checkbox with a quiz on what was their first song, some sections with questions and the possibility to share their experience, and two small curiosities to test the readers’ knowledge. A plug-in is also available to play Pink Floyd’s music on Spotify, and another one to enjoy singing “Wish you were here” with the help of lyrics. Moreover, we added some photos of the band, of their representative logo and of Roger Waters. Finally, we have added a section where the user can contact us to receive further information about our project. The overall visual data is complemented with textual information throughout the whole article.
